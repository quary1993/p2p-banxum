# Documents, Contracting, and E-Signature

Status: Draft. Updated with operating-model, identity, payment, product, origination, marketplace, document, annual tax information statements, accounting/tax, recovery allocation, and KYC evidence-boundary decisions on 2026-05-30.

## Purpose

Define how the platform generates, stores, accepts or signs, versions, controls, and exports documents across onboarding, loan origination, investing, servicing, and compliance.

## Scope

- Document templates.
- Dynamic document generation.
- Checkbox/clickwrap acceptance workflows for v1 investor documents.
- Future e-signature workflows.
- Uploads and evidence files.
- Template versioning.
- Document access control.
- Document retention and deletion.
- Audit evidence packages.
- PDF, CSV, and ZIP evidence package exports where applicable.

## Document Categories

- Platform terms.
- Partial funding consent language in platform terms and investment acknowledgements.
- Investor risk disclosures.
- Generic P2P lending risk acknowledgement document.
- Investment terms and investment acknowledgements.
- Natural-person KYC/AML provider references, statuses, decision metadata, required data fields, downloaded provider reports where possible, raw provider webhook payloads where possible, supporting-document references/local copies where possible, and manual review evidence.
- Local copies of legal-entity lender and borrower KYB/AML onboarding evidence required for regulatory access where uploaded, imported, downloaded, or recorded by admin.
- Borrower/loan opportunity files managed offline and uploaded or referenced by admin where needed.
- Optional borrower presentation PDF.
- Optional borrower financials PDF.
- Optional generic borrower documents uploaded and named by admin.
- Offline credit files or memo, if uploaded later.
- Loan listing disclosures.
- Loan agreement.
- Investor claim assignment agreement or equivalent assignment instrument.
- Secondary-market seller/listing terms.
- Secondary-market assignment or reassignment document.
- Collateral/guarantee documents.
- Collateral/security documents where available; v1 platform fields are collateral type, collateral value, calculated LTV, and optional free-text description.
- Payment instructions.
- Refund notices or evidence for primary-market surplus/full excess payments.
- Report export PDFs and CSVs.
- ZIP evidence packages with manifest where applicable.
- Repayment statements.
- Investor account statements.
- Annual investor statements.
- Borrower account statements generated by admin as PDF and CSV.
- Borrower annual tax information statements generated by admin as PDF and CSV.
- Garanta internal annual account and tax information reports.
- Internal distribution/payment evidence artifacts, including bank statement attachments where admin records them.
- Early repayment, partial repayment, and operational loan change notices.
- Annual lender tax information statements.
- Default/collections notices.
- Complaints and dispute documents.

## Decisions

### DOC-DEC-001: Mandatory Launch Document Set

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / legal / operations.

Decision:
The launch platform must support the full document set needed for primary investments, secondary-market purchases, payments, reporting, and tax outputs.

Mandatory launch documents, when applicable to the workflow:

- Platform terms.
- Investor risk disclosure.
- Generic P2P lending risk acknowledgement document.
- Investment terms and investment acknowledgements.
- Loan claim assignment document per investment order.
- Secondary-market seller/listing terms when listing a holding.
- Secondary-market assignment/reassignment document per secondary-market purchase.
- Payment instructions.
- Investor statements and repayment statements.
- Annual lender tax information statement for all investors.
- Borrower account statement PDF/CSV generated by admin.
- Borrower annual tax information statement PDF/CSV generated by admin.
- Garanta internal annual account and tax information report.
- Refund notices or refund evidence where needed.
- Default/recovery or operational-change notices where needed.

Rationale:
The document set must cover the full investment lifecycle, including primary investment, secondary-market transfer, servicing, tax reporting, and exceptions.

Impacted modules:
- Investor Portal.
- Marketplace, Investments, and Allocations.
- Accounting, Tax, and Finance Operations.
- Communications and Notifications.

Follow-ups:
Legal counsel must approve the final wording and whether any document is merged into another document or delivered as a separate PDF.

### DOC-DEC-003: Checkbox Acceptance Instead of E-Signature in v1

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / legal / operations.

Decision:
No e-signature provider is used in v1. Investor-side contracting and acknowledgements use checkbox/clickwrap acceptance at investment time and secondary-market purchase time.

All v1 investor documents that require acceptance are accepted by checkbox/clickwrap. The platform records the accepted document/template version, data snapshot, timestamp, user, IP/user-agent where available, and acceptance context.

Rationale:
Checkbox acceptance is simpler for launch and matches the current operating choice. E-signature integration can be added later if legal or operational requirements change.

Impacted modules:
- Investor Portal.
- Marketplace, Investments, and Allocations.
- Security, Privacy, and Auditability.

Follow-ups:
Legal counsel must confirm which acceptance evidence is sufficient for each document type and jurisdiction.

### DOC-DEC-004: Offline Borrower Documents as Optional Admin Uploads

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta operations.

Decision:
Borrower-side loan agreements, security documents, collateral documents, and borrower-signed documents are handled offline. The platform may store them as optional admin uploads.

Rationale:
Borrowers have no portal and the Garanta-borrower relationship is kept offline, but the platform should be able to store relevant evidence and documents.

Impacted modules:
- Borrower and Entity Records.
- Origination, Credit Review, and Underwriting.
- Admin and Operations Portal.

Follow-ups:
Define which offline borrower documents are internal-only and which, if any, can be investor-visible.

### DOC-DEC-005: Claim Assignment Document Per Investment Order

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / legal / operations.

Decision:
The investor claim assignment document is generated per investment order in v1. This can be changed in a later version if the legal or operational model changes.

Rationale:
Per-order generation creates a clear link between order, accepted terms, assigned claim share, lender, borrower, loan, and effective date.

Impacted modules:
- Marketplace, Investments, and Allocations.
- Investor Portal.
- Payments, Ledger, Custody, and Reconciliation.

Follow-ups:
Define whether partially funded orders generate one final assignment document for the accepted amount or a corrected/replaced version.

### DOC-DEC-006: Pre-Investment Borrower Documents

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / operations.

Decision:
Before investing, eligible logged-in investors can access optional borrower documents when admin has uploaded them and made them available for the listing:

- Borrower presentation.
- Borrower financial PDF.
- Extra generic borrower documents uploaded and named by admin.

If a document is not uploaded or not published, the investor portal must not show an empty label or placeholder.

Rationale:
This supports flexible disclosure without making every borrower file mandatory.

Impacted modules:
- Investor Portal.
- Borrower and Entity Records.
- Origination, Credit Review, and Underwriting.

Follow-ups:
Define default visibility for generic borrower documents and whether each upload has an internal/public flag.

### DOC-DEC-007: Investor Document Delivery and Download

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / operations.

Decision:
After investing, investors can download their accepted/generated documents from the portal and receive them by email. The same applies to secondary-market purchase documents.

Rationale:
Portal download gives investors durable self-service access, while email delivery provides immediate evidence at the transaction moment.

Impacted modules:
- Investor Portal.
- Communications and Notifications.

Follow-ups:
Define email attachment versus secure-link delivery and file-size limits.

Implementation status:
Generic acceptance-document download is implemented for PDF and CSV. Each render is regenerated from immutable template version plus acceptance data snapshot, returns a checksum/manifest, records the renderer version used for byte-level traceability, and stores append-only rendered-artifact metadata without storing the generated file bytes as the source of truth. Production still requires Garanta-approved template content before real transactions.

### DOC-DEC-008: Regenerate PDFs From Templates, Generate at Transaction Time

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / engineering.

Decision:
Generated PDFs are regenerated on the fly from templates and the stored transaction data snapshot. At primary investment time and secondary-market purchase time, the platform generates the relevant PDFs and sends them to the investor by email.

The primary record is the acceptance event, template version, and data snapshot. Generated PDF files do not need to be stored permanently as the source of truth, though delivery metadata and any generated/sent artifact references should be auditable.

Generated PDFs and CSVs, where applicable, are required for launch. Clickwrap evidence plus an email confirmation alone is not sufficient as the production document-delivery model; the platform must provide downloadable generated artifacts for accepted/generated documents, statements, tax information, and transfer/assignment evidence where applicable.

Rationale:
Regeneration avoids storing redundant generated files while preserving reproducibility through template versions and immutable transaction data snapshots.

Impacted modules:
- Investor Portal.
- Communications and Notifications.
- Security, Privacy, and Auditability.
- Infrastructure, DevOps, and Platform Operations.

Follow-ups:
Define whether regulator/auditor exports require materialized PDFs to be generated and stored in evidence packages.

Implementation status:
Generic template-driven acceptance evidence PDFs and CSVs are implemented. The renderer replaces approved template variables from the stored acceptance data snapshot, rejects missing template variables instead of silently emitting incomplete legal documents, neutralizes CSV formula cells, records append-only `DocumentRenderedArtifact` metadata with checksum/manifest and renderer version, and exposes a self/admin-scoped artifact API. Production layout polish and final legal wording remain separate tasks; the current renderer is a deterministic backend artifact generator, not the final WeasyPrint visual template engine.

### DOC-DEC-009: Template Change Definition

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / legal / superadmin.

Decision:
A template change means modifying the reusable document template itself, including legal clauses, standard wording, document title, layout, required checkboxes, dynamic variables, jurisdiction wording, user-class wording, or product-specific sections.

A template change does not mean changing loan-specific or investor-specific data that is inserted into a generated document from the approved data snapshot.

Rationale:
Template changes can alter legal/compliance meaning, while generated document data changes are governed by the underlying loan/order data workflows.

Impacted modules:
- Admin and Operations Portal.
- Security, Privacy, and Auditability.

Follow-ups:
Define whether offline legal review evidence must be attached before superadmin publishes a new template version.

### DOC-DEC-010: Superadmin Owns Template Changes

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / legal / superadmin.

Decision:
Superadmin can create, update, configure, and publish reusable document template versions.

Rationale:
Template changes affect platform terms, investor disclosures, assignment language, reminders, penalties, and regulated communications. They belong to the parametrization/configuration layer rather than day-to-day admin operations.

Impacted modules:
- Admin and Operations Portal.
- Communications and Notifications.
- Security, Privacy, and Auditability.

Follow-ups:
Define whether superadmin template publication requires uploading offline legal approval evidence.

### DOC-DEC-011: Terms Acceptance Categories

Status: Accepted.
Date: 2026-05-20.
Owner: Garanta product / legal / compliance.

Decision:
The platform has four terms/acceptance categories:

- Registration-time terms accepted during account registration before KYC completion and before dashboard/deposit access.
- Primary-market investment terms accepted when investing in a primary-market loan.
- Secondary-market buyer terms accepted when buying on the secondary market.
- Secondary-market seller/listing terms accepted when listing a holding for sale on the secondary market.

All launch acceptances use checkbox/clickwrap evidence.

Exact checkbox labels and acknowledgement wording for primary investment, secondary-market purchase, and secondary-market listing remain generic at planning stage and must be finalized through legal/template drafting.

Rationale:
Different workflows require different legal acknowledgements and acceptance evidence, especially once user balances, withdrawals, reinvestment deadlines, penalties, and secondary-market actions are supported.

Impacted modules:
- Investor Portal.
- Marketplace, Investments, and Allocations.
- Identity, KYC, KYB, and AML.
- Communications and Notifications.

Follow-ups:
Draft the four template groups, exact checkbox labels, exact acknowledgement wording, and decide whether any acknowledgements are repeated per transaction.

## Template Requirements

- Templates are versioned.
- Template changes are changes to reusable standard wording, clauses, layout, variables, or acceptance language, not changes to transaction data inserted into a generated document.
- Template changes are superadmin-owned.
- Generated documents reference template version and data snapshot.
- Accepted/generated documents are reproducible from template version and data snapshot.
- The same accepted document can be regenerated from the same template version and data snapshot. If the template or accepted data snapshot changes, the corrected document must be generated as a new version.
- Launch document language is English.
- Jurisdiction, product type, and user class drive template selection where needed.
- V1 uses generic P2P lending risk acknowledgements rather than a detailed suitability questionnaire. The final legally approved risk acknowledgement/risk disclosure document must be created and uploaded into the software before production use. Planning docs may use generic placeholder wording only.
- Assignment documents must reference the lender's pro-rata share, the underlying loan claim, the borrower, and the effective date.
- Investment terms must disclose that admin may proceed with partial funding case by case and that lender notification is sufficient if this is covered in the accepted terms.
- Investment terms must disclose that pending orders do not reserve loan capacity until balance/funds are allocated, investors cannot cancel orders, and excess or non-proceeding payments may be returned or credited to investor balance according to the selected money-flow rule.
- Loan/investment terms must disclose repayment allocation, partial repayment, early repayment without fee, day 5 late status, day 16 default status, Europe/Zurich day-counting for those statuses, and notification-only operational loan changes where legally acceptable.
- Secondary-market transfer documents must reference the seller, buyer, transferred holding, current principal balance, sale price, discount/premium, accrued interest, maker/taker fees, buyer total cost, seller net proceeds, effective servicing transfer date, and that only the full holding is transferred.
- Secondary-market seller/listing terms must disclose that the secondary market is a bulletin-board claim/participation transfer mechanism, not a regulated trading venue; partial sales or partial transfers of one holding are not allowed.
- Secondary-market buyer terms must disclose that accrued interest up to settlement belongs to the seller and future interest after settlement belongs to the buyer.
- Non-standard secondary-market listing documents and buyer acceptance flows must include the admin disclosure note and additional buyer risk acknowledgement for late, overdue, restructured, under-observation, default, recovery, legal-enforcement, payment-incident, or otherwise non-performing/non-standard holdings.
- Registration-time terms must disclose the balance model, allowed currencies, FX service, the rule that FX conversion does not reset 30/60-day balance-ageing timers, target-currency deadline inheritance from consumed source balances, balance ageing rules, FIFO consumption, withdrawal/reinvestment deadlines, day-60 forced-withdrawal/penalty rule, missing-IBAN financial-action freeze with read-only access preserved, and the regulatory non-extendability of the 60-day holding limit.
- Annual lender tax information statement templates must support calendar-year output for all investors and include interest received/credited, fees paid, FX costs/fees, potential losses after final default resolution where advisor-approved, recoveries, recovery category split, secondary-market results, balance penalties if any, and information-only principal/balance movements.
- Borrower account statement and annual tax information statement templates must support admin-generated PDF and CSV outputs because borrowers have no portal. Tax summaries include interest paid, Garanta fees, administrative costs, FX costs, contractual/default/penalty interest where applicable, penalties/recovery costs where applicable, and information-only principal received/repaid/recovered/outstanding sections.
- Garanta internal annual tax information report templates must extract platform revenue and platform costs while keeping client-money/settlement movements separate from Garanta income and costs.
- Annual tax information statement templates must state that they are informational only and not tax advice, and that final tax treatment remains the responsibility of each party.

## E-Signature Requirements

No e-signature provider is used in v1. All investor-side acceptance is checkbox/clickwrap acceptance.

Launch checkbox/clickwrap evidence should record:

- User.
- Timestamp.
- Document/template version.
- Data snapshot or snapshot reference.
- Accepted checkbox labels.
- Acceptance context, such as primary investment or secondary-market purchase.
- IP address and user-agent where available.

Future e-signature requirements, if introduced later:

- Identify signer and signing authority.
- Support individual and multi-party signing.
- Support signing order.
- Support evidence certificate.
- Support declined/expired signatures.
- Support document tamper evidence.
- Support API integration and webhooks.
- Meet legal requirements for the relevant document type and jurisdiction.

## Access Control

- Investors see documents relevant to their investments and disclosures.
- Investors can download accepted/generated investment and secondary-market documents after the relevant transaction.
- Investors receive generated investment and secondary-market documents by email at transaction time.
- Before investing, eligible logged-in investors can access borrower presentation, borrower financial PDF, and admin-named generic borrower documents if admin has uploaded and published them for the listing.
- Borrower portal access is out of scope; borrower documents are managed by admin after offline Garanta-borrower handling.
- Borrower-side signed/offline documents are optional admin uploads and can be internal-only unless admin/legal marks a document investor-visible.
- Staff visibility depends on role.
- KYC/KYB data, provider references, downloaded reports, raw provider webhook payloads, identity/KYB evidence, and manual-review decisions are more restricted than standard loan documents.
- Didit performs the launch natural-person KYC capture/check flow. The platform stores provider references, statuses, decision metadata, required KYC data fields, downloaded provider reports where possible, raw provider webhook payloads where possible, supporting-document references/local copies where possible, and audit evidence.
- Legal-entity lender and borrower KYB/AML documents stored locally must be available to Garanta for regulator access and audit evidence, with Swiss-controlled storage, restricted access, encryption, and retention controls.
- Closure-time privacy pseudonymization must preserve documents and evidence. Generated documents, uploaded documents, KYC/KYB/AML evidence, contracts, statements, reports, and audit artifacts are not destructively modified by the closure pseudonymization workflow; access remains controlled through normal retention and restricted-access rules.
